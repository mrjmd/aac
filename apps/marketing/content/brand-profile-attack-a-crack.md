# Brand Profile — Attack A Crack

## Business

- **Name:** Attack A Crack
- **Tagline:** We Seal Cracks & Secure Your Home
- **Industry:** Residential foundation repair
- **Location:** Greater Boston / South Shore, Massachusetts
- **Phone:** 617-668-1677
- **Website:** attackacrack.com

## Services

- Basement crack repair (epoxy & polyurethane injection)
- Foundation waterproofing
- Structural crack assessment
- Bulkhead / hatchway sealing
- Sump pump installation
- Interior drainage systems
- Radon mitigation (sealed sump)

## Target Audiences

- **Primary:** Homeowners in eastern Massachusetts who have discovered or suspect a foundation issue — water intrusion, visible cracks, musty basement smell
- **Secondary:** Real estate agents needing pre-sale foundation assessments or buyer-side inspections
- **Tertiary:** Property managers of multi-family homes with recurring basement water issues

## Voice & Tone

- **Voice:** Knowledgeable neighbor — expert but not academic, direct but not pushy
- **Tone keywords:** confident, helpful, no-nonsense, approachable, educational
- **Personality:** Like the contractor who explains what they're doing and why, doesn't upsell, and texts you back the same day
- **Reading level:** 8th grade. Short sentences. Active voice.

### Phrases to Use

- "Call or text" (never just "call")
- "Free quote" (not "estimate" or "consultation")
- "Text us a photo" (signature CTA — differentiator)
- "Seal cracks" / "secure your home"
- "New England homes" / "Massachusetts homeowners"
- "Before it gets worse"
- "Foundation repair done right"

### Phrases to Avoid

- "Don't wait" / "Act now" / "Limited time" (no manufactured urgency)
- "Cheap" / "Affordable" (compete on quality, not price)
- "We're the best" (show, don't tell)
- "Waterproofing company" (too generic — we're crack repair specialists)
- "Mold" (we don't do mold remediation — mention moisture/dampness instead)
- "Guarantee" without qualification (legal risk)
- Jargon without context: "hydrostatic pressure" alone — always pair with plain English

## Content Pillars

1. **Educational** — How foundations work, why cracks form, seasonal risks, soil behavior, drainage basics. Goal: position as the knowledgeable expert.
2. **Showcase** — Before/after photos, job walkthroughs, "crack of the week" personality posts. Goal: demonstrate quality and real results.
3. **Seasonal/Weather** — Rain, freeze-thaw, spring thaw, hurricane season, winter prep. Goal: timely relevance tied to when people notice problems.
4. **Testimonial/Social Proof** — Customer quotes, review highlights, "just finished" celebrations. Goal: trust building.
5. **Personality** — Behind-the-scenes, team moments, humor, local community. Goal: humanize the brand, stand out from generic contractors.
6. **Blog Promotion** — Tie social posts to blog articles with link-back. Goal: drive traffic, reinforce SEO content.

## Visual Identity

### Colors

- **Primary (AAC Blue):** #1e6fb8 — top bars, accents, links, logo background
- **Accent (AAC Yellow):** #f0c34b — CTAs, highlights, callout box borders, badge fills
- **Dark:** #1a1a1a — body text, headings, borders, hard shadows
- **White:** #ffffff — backgrounds, text on dark/blue surfaces
- **Light Gray:** #f5f5f5 — page backgrounds, subtle dividers

### Color Rules

- Yellow + dark text (#1a1a1a) — never yellow + white
- Blue + white text — always
- Hard shadows use solid #1a1a1a, not transparent/diffuse

### Typography

- **Headlines:** Space Grotesk, weight 900 (black), uppercase, tight tracking
- **Body:** Inter, weight 400–700, sentence case
- **CTA buttons:** Inter or Space Grotesk, weight 900, uppercase, widest tracking, 11px
- All text must be readable at Instagram grid thumbnail size (~110px square)

### Logo

- **File:** logo.jpg (blue background version is primary)
- **Description:** "ATTACK A CRACK" in white cracked/distressed concrete texture with dual yellow lightning bolts crossing through. Small "-A-" between them. Blue background (#1e6fb8).
- **Placement:** Bottom-center for most templates, bottom-right for photo-only posts
- **Size:** 140–160px square for Instagram posts
- **Treatment:** Always on blue background with subtle backdrop for readability on any surface

### Design Language — Neo-Brutalist

- **Hard shadows:** solid offset (e.g., 6px 6px 0 #1a1a1a on CTAs, 4px 4px on cards)
- **Thick borders:** 2px solid #1a1a1a on buttons, cards, template elements
- **Rounded corners:** rounded-xl (16px) consistently
- **Hover lift:** translate-y -2px on interactive elements
- **High contrast:** no subtle grays in templates — dark text, bold colors

### Photography Style

- Photorealistic, editorial quality
- Natural light preferred; interior: bright; exterior: golden hour or overcast
- Neutral to warm color temperature — avoid cold/clinical blue
- New England setting: colonial, cape, ranch homes; snow, foliage, coastal towns
- Professional but approachable — show the problem AND the solution
- Avoid: over-saturated colors, scary disaster imagery, cartoon style, AI text artifacts

## Post Templates

Nine template patterns catalogued from existing Instagram/Facebook output. Templates are
listed in priority order for the image pipeline (build first → build later).

### Template A: Headline Bar + Callout Box (Workhorse)

- **Layout:** Three-zone vertical stack on photorealistic background
- Zone 1 (top): white/light bar with bold black headline, all caps
- Zone 2 (middle): yellow-bordered rounded callout box, white fill, dark explanatory text
- Zone 3 (bottom): large logo
- **Best for:** Most content types — educational tips, seasonal warnings, general posts
- **Example headline:** "HEAVY RAIN TESTS YOUR FOUNDATION"
- **Example callout:** "South Shore storms can increase basement water pressure"

### Template C: Dark Header + Photo (Bold Statement)

- **Layout:** Dark top bar with gold text, photo below, logo at bottom
- Top bar: black background, gold (#f0c34b) uppercase text, two lines
- Photo area: real or AI photo of foundation, repair, or home exterior
- **Best for:** Bold value propositions, seasonal warnings
- **Example line 1:** "PREVENTION BEATS REPAIR"
- **Example line 2:** "Early action saves time and stress"

### Template H: Carousel Slide 2 (Educational Payoff)

- **Layout:** Black/dark background with white centered text
- Pairs with any slide-1 template (A, C, D, F) as second carousel slide
- Smaller, more readable text explaining the concept from slide 1
- **Best for:** Any educational post — slide 1 hooks, slide 2 educates
- **Example:** "Freeze-thaw cycles expand and contract the soil around your home, putting pressure on foundation walls over time."

### Template D: Yellow Badge on Photo (Eye-Catching)

- **Layout:** Large yellow filled shape overlaying a photorealistic background
- Badge: yellow rectangle/banner with dark bold text, ALL CAPS
- Background: real photo relevant to the message
- **Best for:** Single-message attention-grabbing posts
- **Example badge text:** "WATER AROUND YOUR FOUNDATION MATTERS"

### Template G: Checklist Card (Actionable)

- **Layout:** Blue rounded card overlaying a structural photo background
- Card: blue (#1e6fb8) with white title, dotted separator, checkbox items
- Background: dimmed photo of basement or structure
- **Best for:** Seasonal inspection guides, listicles, tip lists
- **Example title:** "SPRING CHECKLIST"
- **Example items:** "Wall or floor cracks", "Basement dampness", "Sticking doors"

### Template F: Photo + Direct Text Overlay (Clean)

- **Layout:** Photorealistic scene with large white text directly overlaid, no boxes
- Optional small yellow accent (underline, sparkle)
- Logo absent or very subtle
- **Best for:** Clean, magazine-style posts
- **Example:** "COLD WEATHER CAN EXPOSE HIDDEN CRACKS"

### Template B: Illustration + Split Text (Educational, future)

- AI-generated illustration or cross-section with text zones
- Best for geological/structural concepts

### Template E: AI Scene + Bottom Callout (Story-Driven, future)

- AI scene top 70%, white callout box bottom 30% with logo + narrative text

### Template I: Labeled Diagram (Technical, future)

- Dark header bar + AI scene with annotation labels pointing to features
